ELDERS

 

We’re just at the start of a pastoral vacancy - we’re now looking for our new pastor.

Our four elders now have responsibility for the Fellowship - if you’ve any questions or thoughts, please speak to any one of them.


ALASTAIR CLUNIE

Alastair has been involved with the church since 2002. Professionally, he works for BMS World Mission as Church Relations Manager, having previously worked in both further and higher education.

Alastair wants to see the church grow as a vibrant community of believers with a heart for mission. Outside of church he serves the local community through his role as Chair of Governors at Charlton Primary School.

An enthusiastic photographer, Alastair is a member of The Royal Photographic Society holding their LRPS distinction. He's married to Jan and together they have three grown up children and two grandsons.


EDWIN PENMAN


Edwin was born in Dunfermline, Scotland and became a Christian at a young age. He was baptised in his early teens, already beginning to sense a call to ministry.


Following a few years in the electronics industry, he trained for ministry at London Bible College and was called as Youth Director to International BC in Aberdeen (1991). He completed a year at the Scottish Baptist College (1995) and then moved to SW England, to Taunton Baptist Church as Associate Minister (1996) before joining BMS World Mission as Area Co-ordinator for the SW area (2004).


He’s now working in BMS’ Didcot office as Church Relations Co-ordinator.

Married to Alison, they have two boys. His interests are young people, mission, cars, rugby… and Libby, a black and tan cocker spaniel!


ELSPETH SMITH


A strong Christian family background meant that Elspeth came to faith during her childhood. Through the years her faith has been nurtured and developed in many ways and equally, she delights to see how God works in others’ lives for our mutual encouragement and strength.

Married to Andy now for many years, they have two grown up children, Graham and Kathryn, and a lovely daughter–in-law, Graham’s wife, Catriona.

Working as a Home Economist for Miele, food and cooking are something of a passion but Elspeth also relaxes with a good book, some needlework or enjoy scatching up with friends when possible.



MARK CRAIG - Mark IS TAKING A break  FROM HIS ELDER DUTIES UNTIL NEXT SUMMER


Mark has been a full part of the church since 2005, and an elder for nearly 10 years. He is Director of Communications for BMS World Mission, overseeing a team of 20 creative colleagues.


With an active interest in the how the church engages with culture, Mark reviews music, film, books and other media for several Christian networks.


Mark is a National Leader of Governance, in the education sector. He is an adviser in governance to several bodies, including the Church of England.


He is married to Fiona; they have one son, Dan, who is married to Emilia.
