Hope Home, Thailand

 

Kathryn Smith has just been to Hope Home to undertake a BMS medical placement as part of her nursing degree.


She was working with Judy Cook, who founded Hope Home in Chiang Mai, in Northern Thailand to look after children with disabilities.

Hope Home




 

Kathryn reports:

I have just spent three weeks caring for the children at Hope Home, Chiang Mai, as part of my nursing elective. Hope Home is a foster care home for children with disabilities which provides love, security and acceptance in country where this might not be found if you have a disability.

There are six children living at the home aged 2 to 7 yrs with varying degrees of disability ranging from cerebral palsy to visual impairment to learning disability. The staff were very welcoming and I was made to feel part of the team even though they spoke little English and I spoke even less Thai. This lead to some tricky situations, however, I found that a smile goes a long way.

I was involved in providing daily care and activities for the children. This involved feeding the children, giving them their medicines and physiotherapy plus taking them out on their daily walks. We, also, took them swimming (which was bliss in 40˚c heat) and we had the opportunity to visit one of the children’s family in the Hill Tribes outside of Chiang Mai.

I had an amazing time in Hope Home where God opened my eyes to the trouble these children could have faced if Hope Home was not there to care for them. I would just like to take the opportunity to thank you for all your support and prayers it meant a lot to me while I was out there.
